### Release Report Personal Finance Tracker - Version 1.0.0
---

**Overview**

The Personal Finance Tracker provides the user with a graphical interface for managing income and expenses. The user can enter custom categories and create transactions to keep track of their spending. There is a feature for filtering all transactions by name, amount, entry type, and category. In addition, they can create personalized account and category specific goals related to their finances. Once data has been generated, the user can visualize the data in graphs on the reports page. Users can see trends for weekly, monthly, and yearly time periods as well as see what their finances are distributed in pie charts.


## Development Highlights

**Project Initiation:**

The project creation began in August 2025 with Group 7 voting on their preferred topic from a given list. Once a topic had been chosen, they brainstormed ideas in a kickoff meeting and discussed each member’s developing strengths and experience. The initial plan was to meet three times a week and a fourth time during retrospective weeks.


**Requirements Gathering:**

Each member inputted information on what they believed a user would expect from a finance tracking application. A list of requirements was compiled, and they were expounded through individual research. Also, review of the previous cohort SRS document provided insight into overall themes and developmental uses. The team prioritized user stories and developed tasks to accomplish the stories listed on the Trello board.

**Design and Architecture:**

The design was based around a new user without an account being able to visit the site, create an account, and begin building their personalized finances to budget over time. The site is simplified and user friendly to those without a lot of computer literacy. The architecture was predetermined using visual aids to structure the layout.


## Development Progress:

The project was completed in numerous sprints that lasted two weeks long. During each sprint, the team met collectively multiple times a week, worked individually outside of meetings, and focused on meeting deadlines. Deliverables were expected each week and were mostly met aside from completing any complex features.
