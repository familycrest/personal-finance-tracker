**Introduction**

This application enables a user to easily balance their income and expenses on a graphical user interface. Users can track budgetary goals based on chosen categories with complementary graph displays showing transaction history. The ability to filter transactions also demonstrates the application’s sorting and data management features. This app is for anyone with basic computer literacy and finances to manage.

**Getting Started**

The minimum system requirements include a modern web browser (Chrome, Firefox, or Edge), JavaScript enabled, 4 GB of RAM, and a stable internet connection.

The application can currently be accessed [here](https://matrix.skybldev.eu.org).

## Using Personal Finance Tracker

### Creating an Account

1. Access the website.
2. Click "Sign Up" on the top bar of the page.
3. Enter your login details - a username, email, and password - then submit.
4. You will be asked to verify your email address. Visit your inbox and wait for the authentication code from us to arrive.
5. Enter the authentication code, and hit submit. You've now successfully signed up!

### Logging In to Your Account

1. Access the website..
2. Click "Login" on the top bar of the page.
3. Enter your email address and password.
4. You will be asked to verify your email address. Visit your inbox and wait for the authentication code from us to arrive.
5. Enter the authentication code, and hit submit. You will then be logged in.

### Your Transactions

To **access** your transactions, click "Transactions" on the top bar of the page. Here, you can see all of your transactions, with the latest ones near the top.

To **add** a transaction, fill out the input values in the Add Transaction form on the left. From here, you can either confirm by clicking "Add", or start over by clicking "Reset".

Below that form, you can **filter** the transactions shown on the table. Enter criteria such as the date, name, and category, then click "Apply Filters" to apply or "Reset" to clear them.

To **edit** a transaction, select the transaction you wish to edit, and click "Edit" on the right side of the page. The Edit Transaction form on the left will allow you to update its values. Finally, you can click "Apply" to apply the edit, "Reset" to return to the current version of the transaction, or "Cancel Edit" to cancel it altogether.

To **delete** a transaction, select the transaction you wish to delete, and click "Delete" on the right side of the page. The page will then ask you for confirmation.

### Your Reports

To **access** your reports, click "Reports" on the top bar of the page. Here you will see five charts that give you insight into your earnings and spending. You can also select different time periods to only show data for those time periods.

### Categories

To **acccess** your categories, click "Categories" on the top bar of the page. Here you will see a grid of categories into which you can classify your transactions. These will also show any goals connected to them.

To **add** a category, click "Add Category" on the top-right side of the page. You will them be prompted to enter the category's name and its entry type. Categories with an "Income" entry type will only accept Income transactions, while those with an "Expense" entry type will only accept Expense transactions.

To **edit** a category, click the "Edit" button on the bottom of its card. You will only be able to rename the category from here.

To **delete** a category, click the "Delete" buttom on the bottom of its card. The page will then ask you for confirmation.

### Goals

To **access** your goals, click "Goals" on the top bar of the page. Here you will see two tables: Acccount Goals and Category Goals. Account goals apply to all transactions under an account, while category goals apply only to the transactions under a specific category. This means that, for example, you can set a goal to limit a certain category of  expense, or a goal to earn a certain amount of money from a specfic income source.

To **add** a goal, click the "Add" button under the type of goal you would like to add. You will then be prompted to enter its name, description, entry type, the length of time it applies to, and its amount. For a category goal, you will first have to choose which category the goal is connected to.

To **edit** a goal, first select a goal, then click "Edit" on the table the goal is in. On both account goals and category goals, you will not be able to change the time period the goal applies to. On category goals, you will not be able to change the category the goal is connected to.

To **delete** a goal, first select a goal, then click "Delete" on the table the goal is in. The page will then ask you for confirmation.

### Goals History

To **access** historical information about your goals, click "Goals History" on the top bar of the page. Here you will see a table with your completed goals.

Similarly to transactions, you can **filter** these goals by a number of criteria on the left side of the page. Among these are the date ranges: "Start Date From" and "Start Date To" filter goals that *started* within that range. "End Date From" and "End Date To" filter goals that *ended* within that range.

### Troubleshooting

1. The application won’t start.

+ Verify that the name of the URL is spelled correctly and the internet connection is working. If the issue persists, reload the page and do CTRL + R to force reset the page. Whatever dependency is missing, should be reloaded.

2. There isn’t any data on the Reports page.

+ Verify that you have transactions created on the Transactions page. The data will only populate if you have entries within the past weeks, months, or years.

3. I get an error with the entry type when selecting a category.

+ When a category is created it is associated with an entry type (income or expense). When using the category from the dropdown menu, the transaction entry type must match the category entry type. For example, you cannot have a category called “Food” as an “Expense”, entered in the transactions form as an “Income”.



### Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

1. Is there an amount limit for the transactions?

+ No, you can enter and track as many quantitative amounts as you’d like.

2. Can I edit/add transactions to another user’s account?

+ No, your account is only accessible to you. You cannot change the values in another user’s account.

3. Can I use this app on my phone?

+ Yes, to an extent. Although the application works on mobile divides, it was originally made to be used on a desktop.

4. Does the application save my data?

+ Yes! You will sign up for an account to use the application, so the information will persist in your account.

5. Can I use the application without the internet?

+ No, you must be connected to the internet for the web app to function.
